Where It Began

A small atelier.
An infinite craft.

Kansa was born in 2012 in a modest workshop in New Road, Kathmandu — founded by master weaver Hari Prasad Shrestha and his daughter Meera. What began as a boutique supplying bridal lehengas to local families grew, stitch by stitch, into one of Nepal's most beloved ethnic fashion houses.

The name "Kansa" — derived from the Sanskrit word for bronze — reflects our belief that true beauty is forged through time, patience, and tradition.

Our Values

Craft First

Every garment at Kansa begins with a sketch, not a spreadsheet. We prioritise the integrity of the craft above production volume.

Ethical by Design

We work directly with weavers and dyers across Nepal and Rajasthan, paying fair wages and providing safe, dignified working conditions.

Culture, Celebrated

South Asian textiles carry centuries of meaning. We see our role as custodians — ensuring these traditions are preserved and evolved, not diluted.

Made for You

Every order comes with free alterations and a personal stylist consult, because the perfect fit is not a luxury — it's a right.

Sustainable Practices

Natural dyes, low-waste cutting patterns, and recyclable packaging — our commitment to the planet is woven into everything we do.

Community Driven

10% of every sale supports the Kansa Artisan Fund — providing healthcare, education, and tools to the craftspeople behind our collections.
